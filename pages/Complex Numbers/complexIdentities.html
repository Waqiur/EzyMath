Basic Properties

  1. Commutative law for Addition

    \(z_1 + z_2 = z_2 + z_1\)

  2. Commutative law for Multiplication

    \(z_1 \cdot z_2 = z_2 \cdot z_1\)

  3. Associative law for addition

    \((z_1 + z_2) + z_3 = z_1 + (z_2 + z_3)\)

  4. Associative law for Multiplication

    \((z_1 \cdot z_2) \cdot z_3 = z_1 \cdot (z_2 \cdot z_3)\)

  5. Distributive Law

    \(z_1 \cdot (z_1 + z_3) = z_1 \cdot z_2 + z_1 \cdot z_3\)

Basic Proof

  • The sum of two conjugate complex numbers is real

    Let, \(z = a + ib\) (where \(a, b\) are real numbers) be a complex number. Then, conjugate of \(z\) is \(z' = a - ib\).
    Now, \(z + z' = a + ib + a - ib = 2a\), which is real.

  • If \(z_1\) and \(z_2\) are two complex numbers, then always

    \( |z_1 + z_2| \leq |z_1| + |z_2|\)

  • The product of two conjugate complex numbers is real

    Let, \(z = a + ib\) (where \(a, b\) are real numbers) be a complex number. Then, conjugate of \(z\) is \(z' = a - ib\).
    \(z \cdot z' = (a + ib)(a - ib) = a^2 - i^2b^2 = a^2 + b^2\),
    (Since \(i^2 = -1\)), which is real.

De Moivre's Theorem

The formula for this theorem is:

\((\cos x + i\sin x)^n = \cos(nx) + i\sin(nx)\)
